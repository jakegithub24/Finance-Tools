About Compound Interest

Compound interest is the interest calculated on the initial principal and also on the accumulated interest of previous periods.

The formula for compound interest is:

A = P × (1 + r/n)nt

Where:
A = Final amount
P = Principal amount
r = Annual interest rate
n = Number of compounding periods per year
t = Time in years

Compound interest is often called "interest on interest" and makes a sum grow faster than simple interest.
